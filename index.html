The Problem

South African businesses are losing contracts they should be winning

Every year, thousands of South African QSEs and Generic enterprises lose government tenders, corporate contracts, and procurement opportunities — not because they lack capability, but because their compliance is being managed in spreadsheets, once a year, by an expensive consultant.

"We lost the tender — our certificate had expired"

B-BBEE certificates are valid for exactly 12 months. Without automated expiry tracking, businesses discover their certificate has lapsed when they've already submitted a bid — and are disqualified. This happens to thousands of companies every year.

R1.8M average tender lost per disqualification

"Our consultant charges R25,000 a year and we still don't understand our score"

Most South African businesses pay B-BBEE consultants R15,000–R80,000 per year for an annual scorecard that's out of date the moment it's produced. Between annual cycles, they fly blind. No live score. No early warnings. No improvement plan.

R40,000 average annual consultant cost per business

"We didn't know our Skills Development sub-minimum was at risk"

Missing a sub-minimum threshold means forfeiting ALL points in that element — not just the shortfall. A company spending 1.3% of learneable payroll instead of 1.5% can drop from Level 4 to Level 6 overnight. Without continuous monitoring, you won't know until it's too late.

20 points forfeited if sub-minimum missed

"We can't access the R3M Eskom tender — we're one level too low"

In 2026, pre-qualification criteria require minimum B-BBEE Level 1, 2, or 3 just to enter a bid on the highest-value government and SOE tenders. Without knowing which tenders your current level unlocks — and what it takes to reach the next level — you're leaving millions on the table.

R2.4M average tenders locked per level difference

"We didn't know POPIA required signed consent for race data"

Race, gender, and disability data collected for B-BBEE reporting is classified as "special personal information" under POPIA. Processing it without explicit signed consent from each employee is a criminal offence with fines up to R10 million. Most businesses have no idea.

Up to R10M POPIA fine for non-compliance

"We missed the EE report deadline and got a R1.5M fine"

The Employment Equity Act Amendment Act (2025) introduced tighter deadlines, increased inspections, and higher fines. The EE2 report must be submitted by 1 October each year. Non-submission attracts fines starting at R1.5M for a first offence — and enforcement is intensifying in 2026.

R1.5M fine for first EE non-submission offence

A sub-minimum is a minimum threshold that must be met within a B-BBEE element — typically 40% of the maximum points available for that element. The critical rule: if you miss a sub-minimum, you don't just lose the points for the shortfall. You forfeit ALL points for that entire element.


The most common sub-minimum trap is Skills Development: you need to spend at least 1.5% of your learneable payroll on qualifying training. If you spend 1.3% — just 0.2% short — you lose all 20 Skills Development points. That's the difference between Level 4 and Level 6 for many companies.


ComplyEasy monitors your sub-minimum thresholds in real time and sends red alerts when any element is at risk — giving you enough time to course-correct before year-end. This is one of the most valuable features in the platform and one of the most overlooked by companies managing compliance manually.
